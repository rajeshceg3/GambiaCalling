The Silent Pool

Kachikally

It is a place where time seems to hold its breath. The green water, thick with secrets and duckweed, is home to the ancient ones. They lie still, like logs of driftwood carved by a forgotten hand, watching the world with unblinking, golden eyes. There is a hush here, a reverence that hangs in the humid air, broken only by the rustle of leaves and the soft murmur of those who come seeking blessings. It is a strange, gentle harmony between man and beast, a truce written in the water.

Guardians of the Plains

They stand in the open fields, red-brown sentinels against the blue sky. These laterite pillars have watched the centuries turn, silent and stoic. Who placed them here? Why? The wind blows through the grass, whispering answers that we cannot quite understand. To touch the rough stone is to touch a mystery, to feel the heat of a thousand suns stored within. They are the earth's memory, solidified and standing guard over the bones of kings.
